# Pass 2 — Key References

- **Neural Machine Translation by Jointly Learning to Align and Translate** — D. Bahdanau, K. Cho, Y. Bengio (2015). _introduced additive attention as an addition to RNN encoder–decoder; the Transformer replaces this RNN+attention stack with attention only._
- **Sequence to Sequence Learning with Neural Networks** — I. Sutskever, O. Vinyals, Q. V. Le (2014). _the canonical encoder–decoder LSTM baseline the Transformer improves upon._
- **Convolutional Sequence to Sequence Learning** — J. Gehring, M. Auli, D. Grangier, D. Yarats, Y. N. Dauphin (2017). _ConvS2S is one of the strongest non-Transformer baselines in Table 2; the Transformer beats it on both BLEU and training cost._
- **Long Short-Term Memory** — S. Hochreiter, J. Schmidhuber (1997). _the LSTM that the Transformer explicitly argues it can replace for sequence transduction._
- **Outrageously Large Neural Networks: The Sparsely-Gated Mixture-of-Experts Layer** — N. Shazeer, A. Mirhoseini, K. Maziarz, A. Davis, Q. Le, G. Hinton, J. Dean (2017). _the MoE baseline in Table 2; useful as a contrast against dense attention._
- **Grammar as a Foreign Language** — O. Vinyals, L. Kaiser, T. Koo, S. Petrov, I. Sutskever, G. Hinton (2015). _the constituency-parsing baseline in Table 3; shows the Transformer also beats RNNs on a non-translation task._
